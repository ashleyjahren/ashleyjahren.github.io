The InstructOAR

The InstructOAR is a project designed to help novice rowers learn proper rowing technique efficiently and cost effectively. This was part of a project for Tangible Design Interaction and Learning class. We used Arduino technology to trace where the oar moved in 3D space and then output that to a graph that the user can gain immediate feedback from.

Key Challenges

  • Testing on Dry Land

    We couldn't test on the lake while the crew team was practicing. Testing was supposed to be done in an indoor rowing facility but we had limited time and resources to book the facility.

  • Tracking User Input in 3D Space

    The Arduino was tracking the wrong plane of input. We were looking to output the cyclical motion of rowing from the device. However, we were receiving junk output until we figured out that we were recording the wrong plane of movement. The Arduino was also affected by multiple planes of movement occuring at the same time. We accounted for this by saving each plane of movement in a different directory and outputting to separate graphs.

  • Capturing Proper Rowing Technique

    Every person on the crew team rows a little bit differently. There is one universally accepted method of rowing but it's slightly different depending on your height, weight, and muscular build. We approached this by taking rowing data from people of varying body types to get the widest range of information possible.
